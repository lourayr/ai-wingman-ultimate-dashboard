## WHAT IS AN AGENT? (Critical Concept)

**Simple Definition:** An autonomous bot that makes multiple-step decisions for you without constant human intervention.

**Think of it like:** Hiring a tax expert vs. using a calculator. The calculator (basic AI) does one thing. The tax expert (agent) analyzes your situation, asks questions, researches options, makes recommendations, and completes the work.

**Agent Hierarchy:**
1. **Individual Agent** - Single expert (e.g., research agent)
2. **Agentic Workflow** - Agent following a defined process
3. **Agentic Orchestrations** - Multiple agents working together (like a department)
4. **Multi-Orchestrations/Agentic Teams** - Multiple departments working together (like a whole company)

**Example:** "Research all theaters within 20 miles, find seats no further than row 10, no more than $50, give me 3 options sorted by date."  
A basic AI tool would require you to do each step. An agent does it all.

## AGENTIC FRAMEWORKS (How Agents Work)

**Six Framework Types:**

1. **Single Agent** (Supervisor with tools) - One agent, multiple capabilities
2. **Network** (Hierarchical) - Agents in a tree structure, delegating down
3. **Supervisor** (Custom) - Central coordinator directing specialized agents
4. **Hierarchical** - Formal chain of command
5. **Custom** - Tailored to specific workflow needs
