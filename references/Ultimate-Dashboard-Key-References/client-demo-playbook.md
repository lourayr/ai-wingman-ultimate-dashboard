# Client Demo Playbook

## Pre-Demo Preparation

Before any demo call or meeting, verify these are live and working:

1. Dashboard is accessible at ai-wingman-app.vercel.app
2. Google OAuth is connected (Gmail + Calendar data flowing)
3. Morning Intelligence Panel has today's actual data (not yesterday's cache)
4. Revenue Pulse shows real WooCommerce orders from the last 7 days
5. At least one client intake has been processed (to show the Client Builder in action)

If any module is using mock data, be transparent about it. Say: "This module is currently using sample data; your version would pull from your actual [Gmail/Shopify/etc]."

---

## The 15-Minute Demo Script

### Opening (2 minutes)
Do not start with slides. Do not start with credentials. Start with the dashboard.

"Let me show you what my AI did for me this morning before I opened my laptop."

Open the Morning Intelligence Panel. Walk through:
- "Here is my one priority for today."
- "Here are the three things my inbox needed me to see. The other 40+ emails were sorted and handled."
- "Here is my revenue snapshot. I can see exactly where I am against my monthly target."

The prospect should be thinking: "I want that."

### Pain Discovery (3 minutes)
Now ask questions, but keep them short and tied to what they just saw.

"How many emails did you wake up to this morning?"
"Do you know, right now, what your revenue was yesterday across all your channels?"
"If I asked you what your single highest-priority task is today, would you know without checking three different apps?"

Let them talk. They will describe their own chaos. Take notes.

### Dashboard Walkthrough (5 minutes)
Now show the remaining modules, mapping each to something they just said.

If they mentioned email overload: show the Inbox Manager. "Here is how mine gets triaged. Yours would work the same way with your Gmail."

If they mentioned scattered revenue data: show Revenue Pulse. "This pulls from WooCommerce, Stripe, and my consulting invoices. Yours would pull from [whatever they use]."

If they mentioned team coordination problems: show the Client Operations Builder. "When a new client fills out this intake form, the system automatically generates a diagnostic, scores their operational health, and drafts a roadmap. No manual analysis required."

If they mentioned wasting time on repetitive tasks: show the Automation Marketplace. "These are plug-and-play automations. Appointment reminders, invoice generation, lead follow-up. Each one runs autonomously with guardrails. You toggle on what you need."

### The Math (3 minutes)
This is where the conversation shifts from "that looks cool" to "I need this."

"You mentioned you spend about [X] hours a week on [email/scheduling/invoicing/follow-ups]. At your effective billing rate of [Y], that is [X times Y] per week in time you cannot bill for. Over a month that is [4X times Y]."

"The dashboard costs [$tier price]. The ROI math is not even close."

Pull up the Tool Chaos framework if relevant: "The average small business uses 20-32 tools, opens 30-49 tabs a day, and loses 26-43 hours per week to context switching. Our goal is to get you down to 8-12 tools on one screen."

### Close (2 minutes)
Do not hard-sell. The dashboard already did the selling.

"There are two ways we can move forward. If you want to see exactly where your biggest operational leaks are, we start with the AI Chaos Audit. That is a [$750-2,000] diagnostic where I map your entire tool stack, score your operational health, and hand you a blueprint with specific automations. Most clients then move into a monthly dashboard subscription because the audit reveals enough value to justify it immediately."

"Or, if you already know what you need and want to get started, we can do a half-day build sprint where we stand up your dashboard and activate your first set of automations."

"Either way, the next step is a 30-minute intake call where I learn your specific situation. Want to book that?"

## Post-Demo Follow-Up Sequence

**Within 1 hour:** Send a personalized email referencing 2-3 specific pain points they mentioned. Include a link to book the intake call or audit session.

**Day 2:** Send a short Loom video (under 2 minutes) showing one specific automation that addresses their biggest stated pain point. "I thought about what you said about [X]. Here is exactly how the dashboard would handle that for you."

**Day 5:** If no response, send a brief follow-up. "Wanted to make sure this landed. The audit slot I mentioned is open next [day]. Want me to hold it?"

**Day 10:** Final touch. Share a relevant piece of content (a screen capture of the dashboard doing something impressive, a client result, or a short post about automation ROI). No hard ask. Just value.

If no response after Day 10, move to long-term nurture (monthly content, quarterly check-ins). Never burn the bridge.

## Demo Environment Checklist

Before every demo, run through this checklist:

- [ ] Dashboard loads without errors
- [ ] Morning Intelligence Panel shows today's date and current data
- [ ] Inbox Manager shows recent email triage results
- [ ] Revenue Pulse has data from the last 48 hours minimum
- [ ] Client Operations Builder has at least one completed intake to reference
- [ ] Automation Marketplace shows 5+ skills with ROI estimates
- [ ] Mobile view works (pull up on phone during demo to show responsiveness)
- [ ] Screen sharing is set to the correct window (no personal tabs visible)
- [ ] Talking points customized for this specific prospect's industry/size

## Common Objections and Responses

**"I do not have time to set this up."**
"That is exactly the point. I build it for you. The setup session takes [2-6 hours] depending on the tier, and after that the dashboard runs on its own. Your only job is to check it each morning."

**"I have tried automation tools before and they broke."**
"Most automation tools require you to maintain them. This system has built-in guardrails: if something breaks, it pauses and notifies you instead of failing silently. And the supervised mode means nothing runs fully autonomous until you trust it."

**"This seems expensive."**
"Let us do the math together. [Run the ROI calculation based on their specific hours and billing rate.] If the dashboard saves you even half of what we estimated, the return is [X]x your investment."

**"Can I just use ChatGPT / Zapier / [tool] myself?"**
"Absolutely. And if you have the time and technical comfort to build, maintain, and connect 8-12 different tools into a single workflow, you should. What I sell is the finished product: already built, already running, already connected to your data. The question is whether your time is better spent building the system or running your business."

**"I need to think about it."**
"Of course. While you are thinking, would it be helpful if I ran a quick Tool Chaos Score for your business? It takes 5 minutes, it is free, and it will give you a concrete picture of where your biggest operational leaks are. That way your decision is based on data, not guesswork."
