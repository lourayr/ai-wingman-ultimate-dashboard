# Client Intake Processing Guide

This reference file provides the step-by-step process for analyzing a client intake form, strategic brief, or business assessment and producing Ray's consulting deliverables.

---

## PHASE 1: DATA EXTRACTION

Read the intake form and extract every data point into these categories. If a field is missing, flag it as a diagnostic question for the first session.

### Business Identity
- Business name
- Industry and business model
- Revenue streams (list each)
- Team structure and size
- Years in operation
- Website and digital presence

### Current State
- Tech stack (every tool, platform, subscription)
- Team strengths (what they do well)
- Team gaps (where they need help)
- Current automation (what runs without human intervention)
- Broken or abandoned tools (set up but not used)

### Goals and Constraints
- Primary goal (90 days)
- Dream scenario (3 years)
- Biggest challenge
- Scaling bottleneck
- Investment capacity (budget tier)
- Implementation timeline preference
- AI and automation comfort level

### Client Identity
- Ideal client profile (who do they serve)
- Unique value proposition
- Unconventional approach (what makes them different)
- Success metrics (what does "better" look like)

## PHASE 2: FIVE-CATEGORY SCORING

Score each category 1-10 based on the data extracted. Use these rubrics:

### Spaciousness Scoring Rubric
| Score | Indicators |
|---|---|
| 1-3 | Has protected deep work time, manageable calendar, delegates decisions, energy cycles respected |
| 4-6 | Some protected time but frequent interruptions, calendar creep, founder answers too many questions |
| 7-10 | No deep work blocks, back-to-back meetings, founder is single point of failure, always reactive, burnout risk |

### Repetitive Scoring Rubric
| Score | Indicators |
|---|---|
| 1-3 | Minimal manual repetition, core workflows automated, team knows the systems |
| 4-6 | Several manual processes identified, team aware but no one has automated them, some copy-paste between tools |
| 7-10 | Constant manual data entry, status updates done verbally or via manual messages, no automation or broken automation |

### Time Lost Scoring Rubric
| Score | Indicators |
|---|---|
| 1-3 | Reasonable tool stack (under 15 tools), minimal context switching, files findable |
| 4-6 | 15-20 tools, noticeable switching cost, some searching for files, meetings that could be async |
| 7-10 | 20+ tools, 30+ tabs daily, constant searching, duplicate conversations, 20+ hours/week wasted |

### Info Needs Scoring Rubric
| Score | Indicators |
|---|---|
| 1-3 | Clear source of truth, team self-serves most information, dashboard or reporting in place |
| 4-6 | Some gaps, founder answers questions daily, scattered client data, partial documentation |
| 7-10 | No single source of truth, all knowledge is tribal, founder is the encyclopedia, no documentation |

### Automate Scoring Rubric
| Score | Indicators |
|---|---|
| 1-3 | Core workflows automated and maintained, team trusts the automations, clear ownership |
| 4-6 | Some automation in place but underused or partially broken, no one owns maintenance |
| 7-10 | Zero automation, or paid subscriptions collecting dust (Zapier account with 2 broken zaps), everything manual |

---

## PHASE 3: LEVERAGE ANALYSIS

After scoring, identify leverage points using these rules:

**Rule 1: Compound Categories First**
Time Lost and Repetitive compound fastest. Every hour recovered from these categories multiplies across every workday.

**Rule 2: The 80/20 Cut**
Identify the 20% of changes that deliver 80% of the relief. Never propose 15 changes when 3 will do.

**Rule 3: Quick Wins Build Trust**
Start with something implementable in under 1 week. Client confidence compounds when they see results fast.

**Rule 4: Match Client Bandwidth**
A perfect plan the client can't execute is worse than a good plan they can. Design for their real capacity, not yours.

**Rule 5: Consolidation Before Addition**
Reduce the tool count before suggesting new tools. The client is already overwhelmed; adding more tools without removing others increases chaos.

## PHASE 4: DELIVERABLE GENERATION

### Tool Chaos Report (Client-Facing)
Structure:
1. Executive summary (3 sentences: "Here's where you are, here's what it's costing you, here's what we can do")
2. Current State Map (visual or table of all tools by category)
3. Cost and Time Analysis (monthly spend + weekly hours wasted)
4. Five-Category Scores with brief explanation of each
5. Top 3 Leverage Points with expected outcomes
6. Quick Wins (1-week implementable items)
7. Consolidation Blueprint (what to remove, what to keep, what to add)
8. Recommended engagement scope

### 30-60-90 Day Roadmap
Structure:
- Days 1-30: Quick wins, tool consolidation, first automation
- Days 31-60: Dashboard build, workflow redesign, team training
- Days 61-90: Advanced automation, optimization, handoff and documentation

Each item includes: specific tool, specific action, time estimate, responsible party, success metric.

### Internal Strategy Notes (Ray's Eyes Only)
Structure:
- Client readiness assessment (1-10)
- Engagement risk factors
- Upsell opportunities
- Suggested pricing tier with justification
- Notes on client communication style and preferences
- ND considerations if applicable

## SAMPLE DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS

When intake data has gaps, use these during the first session:

**Spaciousness:**
- "Walk me through your typical Tuesday. When do you feel most in control? When do you feel most overwhelmed?"
- "How many uninterrupted hours did you have for strategic thinking last week?"

**Repetitive:**
- "What task does your team do every single day that makes someone say 'there has to be a better way'?"
- "If I watched your team for a day, what would I see them doing that a machine could handle?"

**Time Lost:**
- "If you tracked every minute this week, where would the biggest surprise be?"
- "How many tools does your team open before lunch?"

**Info Needs:**
- "Where do you go to understand how the business is actually doing, right now?"
- "What question does your team ask you most often that you wish they could answer themselves?"

**Automate:**
- "What is the simplest thing that, if it just ran without anyone touching it, would give you back the most time?"
- "Do you have any automation set up that you're not sure is still working?"
